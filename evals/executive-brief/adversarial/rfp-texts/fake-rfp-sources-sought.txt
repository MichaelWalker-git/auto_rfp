Title: Next-Generation Electronic Health Record (EHR) Interoperability Gateway
Agency: Department of Veterans Affairs, Office of Information and Technology (OIT)
Posted: March 5, 2026

SECTION L — INSTRUCTIONS TO RESPONDENTS

L.1 PURPOSE

The Department of Veterans Affairs Office of Information and Technology is conducting market research to identify potential sources for an EHR Interoperability Gateway solution. This notice is issued for planning purposes only and does not constitute a commitment by the Government to issue a solicitation or award a contract.

L.2 BACKGROUND

VA is modernizing its health IT infrastructure to improve data exchange between VA Medical Centers (VAMCs), community care providers, and the Department of Defense (DoD) Military Health System (MHS). VA currently operates 171 VAMCs and over 1,100 outpatient facilities serving approximately 9 million enrolled veterans.

The Interoperability Gateway must support HL7 FHIR R4 standards, enable real-time clinical data exchange, and integrate with VA's Cerner Millennium EHR deployment (Oracle Health) and legacy VistA systems still operational at approximately 40 facilities.

L.3 DESIRED CAPABILITIES

VA is interested in solutions providing:
- FHIR R4 compliant API gateway with support for SMART on FHIR authorization
- Message transformation between HL7 v2, CDA, and FHIR formats
- Patient matching and identity resolution across disparate systems
- Audit logging and consent management per 38 CFR Part 1
- High availability (99.99% uptime SLA)
- FedRAMP High authorization
- Scalability to handle 50 million+ transactions per day

L.4 INFORMATION REQUESTED FROM INDUSTRY

Interested parties should provide the following (limited to 15 pages):
a) Company overview, size standard, and socioeconomic status
b) Description of relevant EHR interoperability experience
c) Technical approach overview for achieving stated capabilities
d) Rough order of magnitude (ROM) timeline for implementation
e) Any potential teaming arrangements or partnerships
