Agency: National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)

Title: Search and Rescue (SAR) Mission Control System Software Support and Maintenance Renewal

1. AUTHORITY
This sole source action is justified under FAR 6.302-1: Only One Responsible Source.

2. NATURE/DESCRIPTION
NASA GSFC requires continued software support, maintenance, and enhancement services for the Cospas-Sarsat Search and Rescue Mission Control Center (USMCC) software system. The USMCC processes distress signals from Emergency Locator Transmitters (ELTs), Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacons (EPIRBs), and Personal Locator Beacons (PLBs), enabling the rescue of approximately 300+ people annually in the United States.

3. ESTIMATED VALUE
$4.2 million over a 3-year base period with two 1-year option periods.
Total ceiling if all options exercised: $7.8 million.

4. CONTRACTOR
SES Space Solutions (formerly O3b Networks)
Sterling, VA 20166

5. JUSTIFICATION
SES Space Solutions developed the original USMCC software under Contract NNG19HP01C and possesses unique institutional knowledge of the system architecture, proprietary algorithms, and operational procedures. Transitioning to a new vendor would:
- Require 18-24 months of knowledge transfer
- Risk mission-critical SAR operations during transition
- Cost an estimated $3.5M in duplicative onboarding

No other vendor has the requisite knowledge of the MEOLUT (Medium Earth Orbit Local User Terminal) processing algorithms or the USMCC operational software baseline.
