1. Background
The City of Sacramento administers a range of resident-facing benefit programs including emergency
rental assistance, utility assistance, and small business grants. Current intake is managed through a
combination of paper forms, email submissions, and a legacy web portal that lacks automated eligibility
screening. The City seeks a vendor to deploy an Al-powered eligibility determination and case routing
system integrated with the City's existing Salesforce Government Cloud environment.
2. Scope of Work
The selected vendor shall provide the following services over the contract period:
Assessment and documentation of current eligibility determination workflows (Discovery Phase, 4
weeks).
Design and implementation of an AI/ML eligibility screening engine capable of processing
structured application data and attached documentation (income verification, lease agreements,
utility bills).
Integration with Salesforce Government Cloud via certified Salesforce APIs.
Natural language processing (NLP) component to parse free-text application fields and extract
structured eligibility data.
Resident-facing status notification system (email and SMS) with multilingual support (English,
Spanish, Vietnamese).
Administrator dashboard with real-time case volume metrics, eligibility decision audit trail, and
configurable program rules.
Data security architecture meeting City of Sacramento Information Security Policy v3.2 and
California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) requirements.
End-user training for City staff and a 60-day post-go-live support period.
3. Conditional Factors and Areas of Uncertainty
POSSIBLE GO — CONDITIONAL FACTORS BELOW
Salesforce Integration Requirement: Proposal must demonstrate a credible Salesforce Government
Cloud integration methodology or identify a qualified subcontractor with active Salesforce certifications.
Offerors without prior Salesforce integration experience should clearly address their approach.
Local Small Business Preference: City of Sacramento awards a 15-point scoring preference to
locally headquartered small businesses (Sacramento County). Vendors headquartered outside
Sacramento County will not qualify for this preference, creating a competitive disadvantage of up to 15
evaluation points.
18-Month Timeline Feasibility: The scope includes Discovery, full implementation, integration,
multilingual notification system, training, and post-go-live support within 18 months. Proposed staffing
plan and schedule must demonstrate this timeline is achievable with the proposed team.
Federal Past Performance Not Required: This is a municipal procurement. Private sector past
performance demonstrating comparable AI/ML and document automation outcomes is directly relevant
and acceptable.
