to certify compliance with the Levine Act. As part of this process, you will need to certify and warrant that

campaign contribution required to be disclosed under the Levine Act in connection with this Agreement
shall be disclosed on the County of San Mateo Levine Act Disclosure Form, a copy of which is available from
the County upon request. Parties are solely responsible for accurate certification and disclosure. The
Levine Act is complex, and this brief description is not legal advice. For example, certain contributions

proposal is selected, you are urged to review the Levine Act and consider consulting an attorney.

SECTION VI - SCOPE OF WORK AND SPECIAL PROVISIONS
6.1
SUMMARY
The County's Technology Services Department (TSD) is soliciting proposals from qualified vendors to implement
Microsoft's Identity and Access Management (IAM) solution to automate onboarding, offboarding, transfers, and
changes to application access as well as system authorization level assignments. The platform was chosen for its
variety of features, alignment with current County licensing schema, and integration of the existing Microsoft
computing stack in use within the County. This implementation is a key component of the County's process
improvement and automation initiatives to reduce manual processes, improve process consistency and efficiencies
and ensure timely provisioning and deprovisioning of user accounts across the County's technology environment.
The County's primary objective is to maximize return on investment by leveraging and integrating Microsoft's
ecosystem to support its operational objectives. This includes the use of Microsoft Entra ID and access management
capabilities such as IAM, single sign-on (SSO), identify services and any related Microsoft solution necessary to meet
the requirements outlined in this solicitation.
Proposals will be fully responsive to the documentation, descriptions, and specifications contained in this RFP. The
successful proposer will be singularly responsible for the proposed solution and will not use subcontractors to
perform the services.
6.2
CURRENT STATE
The County currently utilizes Okta as its enterprise SSO and acts as an adjunct to on-prem Active Directory by
providing certain user provisioning functions and centralized authentication IdP. Okta acts as middleware between
Workday, the County's system of record for County employees, and on-prem Active Directory (AD). Okta also
provides authentication services, including multi-factor authentication (MFA), and serves as the identify provider
for approximately four hundred (400) third-party applications using SAML 2.0. Application access and authorization
across the environment are largely managed through Active Directory security groups, which are used to control
access to both on-premises and cloud-based applications.
Workday functions as the authoritative system of record for onboarding, offboarding, and transferring County
employees between departments. There is limited automation between Workday and downstream systems, with
Okta acting to facilitate selective lifecycle events for employees. Workday provides approximately fifty-seven (57)
user attributes to downstream systems, some of which are submitted by the County personnel coordinators (PRC)
resident in each County department and tasked with submitting employee related information into Workday.
Many of the Workday provided attributes are passive, meaning they have no direct impact to the downstream IdP,
nor are they mandatory. However, some attributes are critical such as the username and cost center ID which
informs Okta on which County Active Directory domain or organizational unit to place the user. While this
Workday automation is only available for full-time County employees, the end-to-end lifecycle of the employee, as
position and attribute changes occur, is not fully automated and therefore numerous manual configurations
accompany any on/offboarding or department transfers.
Contractor and vendor identities do not reside in Workday and are onboarded and offboarded through service
requests, with ServiceNow functioning as the system of record for these users. All contractor/vendor identity tasks
are currently manually configured with information provided by the requester through ServiceNow requests and
acting as the guide for these configuration tasks.
The County operates a hybrid identity model, with on-premises Active Directory synchronized to Azure Active
Directory (Microsoft Entra ID). This synchronization is currently configured primarily to support authentication to
Microsoft 365 and a limited number of cloud-based platforms. It is expected that the respondent will ensure that
all required synchronization enhancements needed to accomplish full functionality as stated in this RFP will be in-
scope of their proposals.
The County is predominately licensed at the Microsoft 365 G3 level. The County currently integrates six (6) forests
and fourteen (14) domains through an extensive trust design. One forest contains nine (9) San Mateo County
domains and is supported directly by TSD and each of the five (5) remaining domains have their own forest and are
considered affiliated yet not directly supported TSD.
