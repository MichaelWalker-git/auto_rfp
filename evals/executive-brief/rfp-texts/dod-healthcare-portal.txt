IDIQ Award Process
The Government intends to award a contract to each "Qualifying Offeror" as
defined in RFO FAR 2.101 and will not be considering price as an evaluation
factor for this solicitation. This is a streamlined method for awarding
Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ) contracts. A Qualifying Offeror
is an Offeror that is determined to be a responsible source, submits a
technically acceptable proposal that conforms to the requirements of the
solicitation, and the contracting officer has no reason to believe would be
likely to offer other than fair and reasonable pricing. The Government
intends to evaluate proposals in accordance with the evaluation criteria in
Section M. This document provides instructions for submitting information
and proposals for consideration by the Government. The Government
reserves the right to award this effort based on the initial proposal, as
received, without discussions.

proposals in response to this solicitation; however, if the Offeror believes

clear, reinforcing the structure of the document.

cause delays when scrolling through electronic documents.
Embedded graphics should be simplified to improve display performance
in digital formats. Recommended practices include limiting colors to
256, avoiding color gradients, and reducing file sizes.

with the evaluation criteria in Section M.
1.3. Use of Automated Tools (including Artificial Intelligence) in Proposal
Evaluation. The Government may use automated tools. including artificial
intelligence (Al)-enabled capabilities. to assist with administrative
processing and evaluation support activities (e.g., organizing proposal
content, identifying required elements. summarizing sections. and
supporting comparison of proposal information to solicitation
requirements). Automated tools will not be the sole basis for any
evaluation rating or award decision. The Government's evaluation results
ratings, and award decision will be made by Government personnel
Offerors remain responsible for ensuring their proposals are complete.
accurate. and clearly written
1.4. The term "Qualifying Offeror", as defined in RFO FAR 2.101, means an

proposal that conforms to the requirements of the solicitation; and (iii) the
Contracting Officer has no reason to believe would likely offer other than
fair and reasonable pricing. "

evaluation factor for this solicitation. However, consistent with RFO FAR
16.505(b)(1)(ii)(E), the Contracting Officer shall consider cost or price as

the IDIQ contracts awarded pursuant to this solicitation.
