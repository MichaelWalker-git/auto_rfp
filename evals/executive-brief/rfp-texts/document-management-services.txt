SECTION I: Introduction
The Transportation Security Administration (TSA), Enrollment Services & Vetting Programs (ESVP), and Contracting and
Procurement (C&P) propose to issue a task order (TO) that restricts the number of sources provided a fair opportunity to be
considered in accordance with Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) 16.507-6(b)(2), FAR Class Deviation 26-04, Rev. 1 for
FAR Part 16.
The procurement request number is PR267503A002, and the Contracting and Procurement's tracking number is JA-2026-04-

Per FAR 16.507, this acquisition is conducted under a multiple award contract.
SECTION II: Description of Action Being Approved
The TSA intends to award a Firm Fixed Price (FFP) interim TO to ILM Corporation of Virginia, Inc. (ILM) under their
General Services Administration's (GSA), Federal Supply Schedule (FSS), Multiple Award Schedule (MAS), Office
Management, Contract Number: 47QSMA19D08P3 to continue the contracted support for Document Management Services
(DMS). The DMS service provides vetting and credentialing services in support of the Security Threat Assessments (STA).
The ESVP mission is to conduct STAs to identify potential threats to the nation's transportation system and critical
infrastructure. The STA includes biometric and biographic based Criminal History Records Checks (CHRC), checks against
intelligence-related databases, and legal status checks.
The ESVP office manages the DMS; the DMS is a functionality within the Vetting and Credentialing System (VCS) and
Technology Infrastructure Modernization (TIM) system that enables ESVP to maintain an electronic file of all
correspondence related to an applicants' case file.

SECTION III: Description of Supplies or Services
The requirement is to provide document correspondence and mailing services in support of the ESVP mission. Specifically,
the services rendered include receiving incoming documents from applicants (that are responding to questions from TSA or
appealing a TSA determination concerning their application), converting documents to electronic format, uploading
electronic documents to the corresponding case file in the VCS or TIM systems and preparing outgoing correspondence for
mailing for the several TSA Program populations. The DMS supports various programs and populations such as the
following;
Hazardous Material Endorsement Program;
Transportation Worker Identification Credential Program;
Aviation Workers Program and General Aviation;
DCA Access Standard Security Program;
Full All Cargo population;
Indirect Air Carrier population;
Certified Cargo Screening Program population; and
TSA PreV@population.
The total estimated procurement value is $3,281,226.14 for a 12-month base and two 6-month option periods.

SECTION V: Description of Fair Opportunity Exception Rationale
The DMS is a solution that enables ESVP systems to maintain an electronic file of all correspondence and documents related
to an applicants' enrollment, vetting and case management, such as an applicant's TWIC Credentialing. The DMS transmits
the applicants' documents, correspondence and other required applicant data, such as images of identity documents sent either
Page 2 of 4
by the applicant to the DMS for transmission to the appropriate TSA system or documents and correspondence sent from the
appropriate TSA system to the DMS for transmission to the TSA applicant based on the program according to TSA interface
specifications.
Both the VCS and the TIM systems interface with the DMS system. The VCS is the system of record for the Aviation
Workers Program (AVW), Certified Cargo Screening Program (CCSP), DCA Access Standard Security Program (DASSP),
Full All Cargo (FAC) Aircraft Operator, General Aviation (GA), the Indirect Air Carrier (IAC), and the HME Threat
Assessment Program (HTAP). The TIM system is the system of record for the TWIC Program and the TSA
PreV®Application Program.
Without this interim contract a break in service would occur with national security implications as this would directly affect
movement of people and commerce. Moreover, this element of the STA is in place to manage and monitor potential threats,
local or national by vetting and providing credentials through the adjudication process to those qualified workers who
transports goods and people across the nation.
If the DMS is inoperable, then the transportation workers and TSA will be unable to receive any correspondence and
documents related to each applicants' enrollment, vetting and case management status. The adjudication process completes a
security threat assessment and provides the outcome for the applicant. Failure to communicate the STA outcome would result
in transportation workers being unable to forward in the adjudication process. Examples of vetted and cleared TSA programs
and populations include TSA PreV® and TWIC Moreover, without adjudication the transportation worker will not be able
to transport goods, work at the airports or airlines, work at the ports, as well as not be able to work at designated critical
infrastructure locations. Lack of enrollment, vetting, and proper credentialing directly affects the TSA mission in safeguarding
the nation's transportation system ensuring freedom of movement of people and commerce.
TSA awarded the new competitive DMISS BPA, but DOMA cannot deliver any documents until they have obtained ATO
from TSA. The first BPA Order under the competitive DMISS BPA has only obligated funding for the ATO and not
processing documents. This interim TO is necessary to continue the DMS support until DOMA is granted an ATO. Therefore,
ILM is the only company with vetted employees with significant experience performing the DMS requirements.
SECTION VI: Contracting Officer's Determination that the Order Represents the Best Value
The Contracting Officer will ensure that the resulting order represents the best value by comparing the ILM quote to the
independent government cost estimate, reviewing historical pricing, and comparing their proposed labor rates against their
competitively awarded GSA Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) labor rates in order to determine the price to be fair and
reasonable.
SECTION VII: Market Research Description
Market research was conducted for this specific interim sole source action and ILM is the only resource capable of providing
the services without a disruption of services.
SECTION VIII: Other Facts Supporting the Fair Opportunity Exception Justification
Seeking an alternate vendor would require TSA to conduct a suitability review of the vendor's employees and complete on-
boarding requirements. The vetting process, on average, takes 60-90 days. In addition, the contractor will be required to get
special systems access, which is provided through contractor support.
SECTION IX: Actions Taken to Remove Barriers to Competition
This TO is needed to bridge the gap between the end of the current ILM TO 70T02024F7503N003 and ATO approval to
DOMA under the DMISS BPA. As the DMISS BPA 70T02024A7503N001 was competed under GSA eBuy as a small
business set-aside and awarded 01 December 2023 there are no barriers to competing this action.
